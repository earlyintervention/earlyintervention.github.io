ASD Early Intervention

A parent-led guide

Although the diagnosis for autism spectrum disorder (ASD) usually happens over 2 years of age, parent-led early intervention strategies can start earlier. This guide contains practical strategies for the earliest years of ASD parenting.

The guide is written for parents who have:

  • ASD or ADHD family history
  • Noticed stereotypical ASD traits in your baby from 6+ months old

Remember: Early intervention is not about suppressing or 'fixing' neurodivergent traits. It is about developing tailored strategies to build more interpersonal connection and social communication skills. Always consult your GP or a paediatrician and early intervention specialists if you have concerns.

Remember: There is no best earliest timeline for early intervention. Each child develops at their own pace and ASD developmental milestones are often non-linear.
